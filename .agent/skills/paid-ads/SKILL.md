# Paid Ads Skill — Meta & Google Ads

## Purpose
Generate complete, ready-to-execute paid advertising campaign strategies for Meta (Facebook + Instagram) and Google Ads, grounded in the business's actual product, pricing, and ideal customer profile.

---

## 1. Pre-Campaign Framework (Always Run First)

Before writing any copy or setting any budget, collect:

### Business Intel Checklist
- [ ] Product/service name and core value proposition
- [ ] Pricing tiers and their target audiences
- [ ] Ideal customer profile (ICP): role, pain, trigger event
- [ ] Geographic target (city, state, national, international)
- [ ] Monthly ad budget
- [ ] Existing conversion assets (landing page, offer, testimonials)
- [ ] Competitors and differentiators

---

## 2. Meta Ads Strategy Framework

### Campaign Hierarchy
```
Campaign (Objective) → Ad Set (Audience + Budget) → Ad (Creative)
```

### Funnel Structure (Always 3 Stages)
| Stage | Objective | Audience | Budget % |
|-------|-----------|----------|----------|
| TOFU | Awareness / Traffic | Cold — Interest-based | 30% |
| MOFU | Lead Gen / Engagement | Warm — Video viewers / Page engagers | 40% |
| BOFU | Conversions | Hot — Retargeting / Lookalikes | 30% |

### Audience Strategy
- **Cold Audiences**: Interest targeting, Behavioral, Demographics
- **Warm Audiences**: Video viewers (50%+), Page engagers (180d), Website visitors (90d)
- **Hot Audiences**: Lead form openers, Cart abandoners, Lookalike (1-3%) of paying customers
- **Exclusions**: Always exclude existing customers from acquisition campaigns

### Copy Framework: PAS (Problem → Agitate → Solution)
```
Hook (3 seconds): State the pain point directly
Problem: Describe the situation they're stuck in
Agitate: Show the cost of NOT solving it
Solution: Introduce the product as the answer
CTA: Single, clear, urgent action
```

### Ad Formats by Stage
- **TOFU**: Reels (15-30s), Carousel (feature showcase)
- **MOFU**: Lead Ads (native form), Video testimonials
- **BOFU**: Single image (direct offer), Story with urgency timer

## 3. Google Ads Strategy Framework

### Campaign Types
| Type | Use Case | When |
|------|----------|------|
| Search | Capture intent | Always — primary campaign |
| Display | Retargeting | After Search is running |
| YouTube | Brand awareness | If video assets exist |
| Performance Max | Automated full-funnel | After 30 days of data |

### Search Campaign Structure
```
Campaign → Ad Group (theme) → 3-5 Keywords → 3 RSAs (Responsive Search Ads)
```

### Keyword Strategy
- **Exact Match**: High-intent terms (buy, hire, pricing, best)
- **Phrase Match**: Mid-intent queries
- **Broad Match (Modified)**: Discovery — use carefully, add negatives
- **Negative Keywords**: Maintain a list from day 1 (jobs, free, DIY, course)

### Ad Copy Formula (Google RSA)
- **Headlines (15)**: Mix benefit + keyword + urgency + social proof
- **Descriptions (4)**: Expand on unique value propositions
- **Rule**: Every ad group → minimum 3 RSAs with different angles

### Quality Score Optimization
1. Keyword ↔ Ad copy alignment
2. Ad copy ↔ Landing page alignment
3. Expected CTR improvement via ad extensions

## 4. Budget Allocation Principles

### Monthly Budget Split (Meta vs Google)
| Channel | Recommended Split | Why |
|---------|-------------------|-----|
| Meta | 60% | Broader audience discovery, lower CPL for B2B services |
| Google | 40% | High intent — closer to purchase decision |

> Adjust split based on search volume data. If search volume is low, increase Meta.

### Testing Budget
- Never spend more than 20% of budget on untested creatives
- Run A/B tests for minimum 7 days or 100 impressions before judging

---

## 5. KPIs & Targets

| KPI | Meta | Google Search |
|-----|------|---------------|
| CTR | >2% | >5% |
| CPL (Cost Per Lead) | Benchmark per industry | Benchmark per industry |
| ROAS | >3x | >4x |
| Conversion Rate (Landing) | >3% | >5% |
| Frequency | <3 per week | N/A |

---

## 6. A/B Testing Protocol

### What to Test
1. **Hook** (first 3 seconds of video or headline) — highest impact
2. **CTA** (verb, urgency, offer)
3. **Audience** (interest A vs interest B)
4. **Creative format** (video vs static vs carousel)

### Rules
- Test ONE variable at a time
- Statistical significance: 95%+ confidence before declaring winner
- Never pause a winning ad — duplicate and iterate

---

## 7. Optimization Loop (Weekly)

```
Monday:    Review metrics → Pause low performers (CTR < 1%, CPL > 2x target)
Tuesday:   Launch new A/B test creative
Wednesday: Expand winning audiences to lookalikes
Thursday:  Update negative keyword list (Google)
Friday:    Review landing page conversion rate → adjust if <2%
```

---

## 8. Output Format

When generating a campaign strategy, always produce:
1. **Campaign Overview** (objective, targeting, budget breakdown)
2. **Ad Copy Library** (headlines, body copy, CTAs for each funnel stage)
3. **Audience Configurations** (cold, warm, hot segments)
4. **KPI Dashboard** (targets, tracking setup)
5. **30-Day Launch Timeline**
6. **Creative Brief** (for design team or AI image generation)
